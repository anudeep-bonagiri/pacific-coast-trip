The shot plan

A practical capture list layered over the shared route. Arrival times and fees are planning estimates; recheck tides, road access, weather, posted rules, and opening hours before travel.

Drone rules for this route

  • ❌ Oregon route parks and ocean shore: no recreational launch during this trip. Oregon’s new restrictions take effect May 1, 2026; the limited Aug 8 open-site list does not include these coastal stops.
  • ❌ Redwood parks and NPS land: no launch or operation. That includes Fern Canyon, Newton B. Drury, Klamath River Overlook, and Battery Spencer/GGNRA.
  • ❌ California park stops: this guide treats Point Lobos, Garrapata, McWay/Julia Pfeiffer Burns, El Matador, and Point Dume as no-launch locations because of preserve rules or district restrictions.
  • ⚠️ USFS and other public land: verify the exact land manager, posted closure, wildfire/TFR status, airspace, and FAA rules at the launch point. Do not assume a highway pullout is legal: Caltrans requires an encroachment permit and does not allow private recreational launches from state highway right-of-way.
No flight over crowds or moving traffic. The pilot needs TRUST, registration/Remote ID when applicable, visual line of sight, and airspace authorization where required.

Filming gear checklist

  • Secure windshield/dash mount for Big Sur and Avenue of the Giants driving sequences.
  • GoPro/side-window mount for coastal cliffs—the ocean is on the right heading south.
  • Waterproof phone case for Elkhorn Slough kayaking and the surf lesson.
  • Wind protection for the lens and microphone; Oregon and Big Sur are exposed.
  • Extra batteries and cards—plan no charging between Garberville and Salinas on Day 4.
Install and test mounts while parked. The driver never adjusts or operates a camera in motion.

Filming notes for the road

  • Morning light and fog are your friend on Days 1–4; marine layer turns ordinary coast and forest frames moody and cinematic.
  • If Rocky Creek’s one-way signal is still active, traffic may clear in waves. Film only from a legal, protected ground position—never enter or fly over the road.
  • Secure the windshield mount while parked for Big Sur Highway 1 and Avenue of the Giants. Use a fixed side-window/door action camera for cliff sections where the ocean is on the right heading south.
  • Waterproof and tether everything for Elkhorn Slough on Day 4 and the surf lesson on Day 6.
  • If you set up only one dedicated filming stop, make it Avenue of the Giants at about 7:30 AM on Day 4.

Recheck before travel: This is a creative field guide, not a live safety or legal service. Weather, tides, wildfire restrictions, airspace, permits, park orders, parking, road controls, fees, and hours can change. The person operating a drone is responsible for current land-manager and FAA compliance.
